Ecosystems & Adaptations

What is an Ecosystem?

An ecosystem is a community of living things and their environment working together. It includes all the animals, plants, and tiny organisms that live in one place, plus all the rocks, water, soil, and air around them.

Key Ideas

  • Ecosystems can be large (like a rainforest) or small (like a puddle)
  • Ecosystems can be on land or in water
  • Everything in an ecosystem is connected
  • When one part of an ecosystem changes, it affects everything else

How Ecosystems Work

In a healthy ecosystem, all the living and non-living things work together. Plants get energy from the sun, animals eat plants, and when things die, they return nutrients to the soil. This cycle keeps going over and over.

Essential Questions

  • How do biotic and abiotic factors interact to make a healthy ecosystem?
  • How do humans impact the health of biotic and abiotic factors in an ecosystem?
  • How do organisms adapt to survive in different environments?

Biotic and Abiotic Factors

Every ecosystem has two main types of factors that affect how organisms live:

🦁

Biotic Factors

Living things in an ecosystem

Examples: animals, plants, insects, bacteria, fungi, and humans

☀️

Abiotic Factors

Non-living things in an ecosystem

Examples: sunlight, water, temperature, soil, air, and rocks

How They Work Together

Biotic and abiotic factors must work together for an ecosystem to be healthy.

  • Plants need sunlight (abiotic) to grow
  • Animals need water (abiotic) to survive
  • Decomposers (biotic) break down dead things to return nutrients to the soil (abiotic)
  • Temperature (abiotic) affects which animals and plants can live in an area

Important Climate Factors

  • Temperature: How hot or cold the area is
  • Precipitation: How much rain or snow falls
  • Latitude: How far north or south a place is from the equator
  • Elevation: How high above sea level a place is

Land Ecosystems

🌳 Rainforest

  • Warm and very wet (lots of rain)
  • Thick vegetation with tall trees
  • Home to jaguars, parrots, frogs, and snakes

🌲 Taiga (Boreal Forest)

  • Cold with coniferous trees (pine and spruce)
  • Snowy winters, short summers
  • Home to moose, bears, and wolves

🌾 Grassland

  • Mostly grass with few trees
  • Moderate temperature and rainfall
  • Home to bison, zebras, and prairie dogs

🏜️ Desert

  • Very hot and very dry (little rain)
  • Few plants, mostly drought-resistant species
  • Home to camels, snakes, and scorpions

❄️ Tundra

  • Extremely cold and frozen most of the year
  • No trees, only small plants and mosses
  • Home to polar bears, arctic foxes, and musk oxen

Water Ecosystems

🌊 Marine Ecosystems

  • Salt water (oceans)
  • The largest ecosystems on Earth
  • Home to fish, whales, sharks, and coral

💧 Freshwater Ecosystems

  • Fresh water (lakes, rivers, ponds)
  • Smaller but very important
  • Home to trout, frogs, turtles, and beavers

What are Adaptations?

An adaptation is a special body part or behavior that helps an organism survive in its environment. Adaptations develop over many generations as organisms with helpful traits survive and reproduce.

💪

Physical Adaptations

Body parts that help animals survive

Examples: thick fur for cold, sharp claws for hunting, long beaks for finding food

🧠

Behavioral Adaptations

Actions or habits that help animals survive

Examples: migrating south for winter, hibernating, hunting at night

Examples of Adaptations

Desert Animals 🏜️

  • Camel: Stores water in humps, light-colored fur to reflect heat
  • Rattlesnake: Hunts at night when cool, hides during hot days

Arctic Animals ❄️

  • Polar Bear: Thick fur and fat for insulation, white color for camouflage
  • Arctic Fox: Changes fur color from brown (summer) to white (winter)

Ocean Animals 🌊

  • Dolphin: Streamlined body for fast swimming, echolocation to find food
  • Fish: Gills to breathe underwater, fins to swim

Forest Animals 🌳

  • Owl: Large eyes to see at night, silent wings to sneak up on prey
  • Squirrel: Sharp claws for climbing, bushy tail for balance

Important to Remember!

  • Adaptations take thousands of years to develop
  • Animals don't choose to adapt—only those with helpful traits survive
  • Different environments require different adaptations
  • Adaptations help organisms get food, stay safe, and reproduce
